Overview

This Pseudolithos migiurtinus is weird little wrinkled wart of a plant and I just love it. As its name suggests (Pseudolithos means "false stone"), it is a dwarf succulent native to Sommalia's rocky arid environments that resembles a small stone or pebble. The leafless stem is knobby and subspherical: the lower half a U-shape while the top rounds out, which reminds me of a muffin.

Growth pattern

At the top of this plant is a set of 4 rows of symmetrical and hexagonal tubercles extending from the central crown in a cross-like tessallation pattern. In each row, in the valley between two large tubercles is a olive-green bud that I assume will turn into a flower.

As it grows, wrinkles between the tubercles deepen into intricate, fractal-like patterns. However, the bottom half of the stem stays relatively smooth as it doesn't have the same wrinkling pattern in between the tubercles.Observing this growth over the past ~8 months really has been a treat.

Size

This plant is tiny, measuring at only 17.86mm wide when I received it on October 21 2024, and it has grown only an additional ~4mm over the past 8 months. I didn't measure its height when I first received it, but it has noticeably grown more taller, with its height at ~23.16mm above the soil line.

Color

When it arrived, the color of the stem was a vibrant lime green color. 8 months later, the quite intense lighting from my T5 light bulbs has changed its color to muted blue-green color. In addition, the flaking of the epidermal skin cells has dulled it into a pleasing blue-green color.
